Podcast turned Book Covers

Book Cover Design

I was tasked to design a book cover trilogy for Podcast episodes from the series Criminal by the astonishing journalist Pheobe Judge. Take a deep dive into my creative process starting from the research phase to final product.

Client | Insctructor Emily Moody
Service | Editorial Design
Skills | Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign

Challenge & Content

Visual Representation

The objective of a book cover is to convey the true essence of a story in a compelling and distinctive manner. A book cover creates the first impression for a potential reader.

Process & Insight

Research

The first stages of my creative process started with listening to one of my podcast episodes, in this case Secrets and SÃ©ances. A huge portion of my research invloved listening to the episode multiple times to pick up on any themes, ideas and keywords. Other sources for my research included searching the web for images that related to a few themes and words I was able to identify while listening to the episode. Themes like physics, fraud, and shattered.

Conceptualize

Upon reviewing my research notes, using a sketch pad I ideated 50 potential book cover outcomes in hopes to find a concept that best fits the theme of the episode. A large portion of my sketches included imagery realted to shattered glass, smoke, and mirrors.

Generate

After filtering through my sketches, I was able to choose 3 of the strongest ideas to explore different aesthetic directions. I experimented with a variety of techniques like photography, graphic typography, and digital illustration. The main objective for this stage was to find the most visually compelling way to express the concepts from my sketches.

Solution

Asses

The last stage for my creative process was to refine the concept that was selected by the class via a critique and translate that concept into two more book covers.

Create

Through this process, I was able to create a well thoughtout and aesthetically appealing book trilogy.
